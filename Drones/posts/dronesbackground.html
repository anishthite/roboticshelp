Drones- A Background

A background, overview, and intro to drones

Posted by Anish Thite on May 12, 2016

What are drones?

"Drones" are a popular term for UAVS, which are Unmanned, Ariel Vehicles. Simply put, they are pilot-less, remote-control operated, flying peices of machienery. UAVs can cover a wide range of vehicles, from the airplane-derived Predator drone used by the US military, to the famous quadcopter availible to the public.

The quadcopter is widely known, and was popularized by companies such as DJI Drones as well as Parrot Drones Quadcopters have 4 arms, each carrying one prop (propeller), connected to one central hub where the power supply, and the flight computer are stored. Other common types of these "copter" drones are tricopters (3 props), hexacopters (6 props), and even dodecacopters (12 propellers) !

Let's discuss the quadcopter more...

Parts of the Quadcopter

To be airborne, a quadcopter needs the following parts:

  • A battery/power supply- to power the drone
  • A frame- the chassis to connect the electronics and power supply to
  • Four props- so that the drone can fly
  • Four motors- to spin the four indivisual props
  • Four motor controllers/ESCs- ESC stands for Electronic Speed Controller. These are the "mediator" between the motors and the flight controllers, and they able to control the speed of the motors as dictated by the flight computer
  • A flight computer (the brain)- interperates the signal sent by the joystick (ex. "make motor 1 go to 100% power") and "tells" the ESC to go what the joystick is "commanding" (ex. it tells ESC 1 to increase power to 100% wich makes motor 1 spin at 100% power)
  • A joystick and radio sender/reciever- the way that the human operator can control the drone
