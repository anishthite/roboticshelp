Chinmaya Mission Robotics Camp (2016)

The Chinmaya Mission Robotics Camp was held on July 25 to July 29, 2016. It was an introductory course in Robotics, focusing on the application of robotics, and the core concepts of robotics. The goal of the camp was to provide a way for students to leearn these concepts of robotics through hands-on learning. The students built, programmed, and tested robots using the Vex Robotics Design System.

Syllabus

Day 1

  • Powerpoint “What is Robotics”,”Introduction to Vex Robotics”
  • Explain what robotics is, aspects of it, High School and Middle School opportunities in Robotics (FIRST vs VEX), and Career opportunities in Robotics
  • Introduce the basics of Vex Robotics, the design system, part collection, and competition, explain parts that we use, and software we use
  • Begin building Clawbot

    • Day 2

  • Continue to build Clawbot, until 4 wheels and no claw are on
  • Test sample code (base only)
  • Explain structural concepts (wheels, gear ratios, motors)

    • Day 3

  • Finish building arm and claw
  • Test whole robot
  • Explain structural concepts (gear ratio, torque)
  • Explain the Cortex, how the Motors work, how joystick sends information to the Cortex
  • Teach coding (remain in small groups)
  • Begin programming- joystick code

    • Day 4

  • Finish joystick programming
  • Begin autonomous programming*

    • Day 15

  • Finish autonomous programming*
  • Game/Competition between the two robots
  • Explain next steps for immersion into robotics
    • * If time allows for these activities
