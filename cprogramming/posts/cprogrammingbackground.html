An intro to C Programming

Background, History and what you need to know about this language

Posted by Anish Thite on May 12, 2016

C was one of the first languages that was poularized.

What is Programming?

Programming is the way in which we can tell a computer, or any other electronic hardware, what to do. It is essentially a long list of instructions.

What does this have to do with robotics?

In robotics, we often use C as a way to control our robot, and to tell it what motors to move, and when. Essentially, we use C to control the robot, and assign tasks for it to do. There are two main types of controlling the robot. We can either control it ourselves, called a driver control program, or allow the robot to control itself, called autonomous. In either case, the programming is very similar. In responce to a certain stimuli, the robot will accomplish a list of tasks, such as moving motors, reading sensors, or assigning values to variables.

There are a variety of stimuli, such as a joystick, buttons, and a variety of sensors (for more information on sensors, browse through our electronics posts). Some common sensors are touch sensors, brainwave sensors, ultrasound (which lets the robots "see") sensors, soundwave sensors, and color sensors.

Driver Control

In driver control, the robot is controlled remotely by a human operator to complete a task. In many robot competitions, a joystick is typically used.

Autonomous

In an autonomous robot, the robot executes a prewritten code, a list of things to do, to perform a task. Advanced programs often have multiple of these "lists" within them and based on sensor readings, the code will tell the robot which "list" to follow.
